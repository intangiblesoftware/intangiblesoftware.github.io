Acadia to Rochester

A 9‑Day New England & Adirondacks Road Trip

Sunday, August 30 – Tuesday, September 8, 2026 · Phoenix → Bar Harbor → Rochester

Travel assumption: flying into Bangor International Airport (BGR) on Sunday and driving straight to Bar Harbor (~1 hour) — that’s why Bar Harbor is a 4‑night stay. If you’d rather overnight near BGR Sunday night and drive in Monday morning, shift one night from Bar Harbor to a Bangor hotel (the Four Points by Sheraton or Hilton Garden Inn Bangor are the easy picks).

Day‑by‑Day Itinerary

Travel Day

Sunday, August 30 · Phoenix → Bar Harbor

All day in transit. Arrive, check in, sleep.

Travel: PHX → BGR (typically 1 stop; ~7–9 hrs door‑to‑door) → ~1 hr drive BGR → Bar Harbor.

Notes

  • Pick up your rental car at Bangor and head east on I‑395 / Rt 1A → Rt 1 → Rt 3 to Mount Desert Island. Stock the car with waters and snacks at the Hannaford in Ellsworth if you’re running past dinner.
  • If you have any energy left for food, Side Street Cafe in Bar Harbor does great takeout lobster rolls until 9pm, or Havana on Main Street is worth a nicer sit‑down.
  • Otherwise — bed. Tomorrow starts early.
Day 1 · Acadia

Monday, August 31 · Arrival warm‑up hike

Shake out the travel legs on a short‑but‑spicy classic.

Driving: None once you’re on the island — short trailhead hop only.

Morning

  • Breakfast in town at 2 Cats (famous biscuits) or Jeannie’s Great Maine Breakfast.
  • Pick up your Acadia park pass at the Hulls Cove Visitor Center on the way to the trail and confirm the Precipice and Jordan Cliffs trail status (peregrine closures usually lift by early September but confirm in person).

Warm‑up Hike

  • Gorham Mountain + The Bowl + Beehive loop — ~3.2 mi, ~650 ft gain, moderate‑to‑strenuous. Start at Gorham Mountain trailhead off the Park Loop Road, summit Gorham for ocean views, drop down to The Bowl (a pretty glacial tarn), and descend the Beehive iron rungs from the top — the safer, downhill direction for a first rung experience — before looping back via Bowl Trail.
  • If the rungs look intimidating, stay on the Bowl Trail for the descent. Same views, no exposure.

Evening

  • Dinner at Havana (Cuban‑inflected fine dining) or Galyn’s (classic Maine).
  • Ice cream from Mount Desert Island Ice Cream (try the salted caramel).
Day 2 · Acadia

Tuesday, September 1 · Cadillac sunrise + Precipice

Alpenglow on top of the island, then iron rungs up a cliff face.

Driving: Two short trailhead hops inside the park.

Sunrise

  • Cadillac Mountain for sunrise — first place in the U.S. to see the sun in fall. Vehicle reservation required; book on recreation.gov up to 90 days ahead. Arrive 30 min before sunrise; bring warm layers.
  • Back to town afterward for a real breakfast at 2 Cats and a coffee refill.

Main Hike — Strenuous

  • Precipice Trail → Champlain Mountain → Bear Brook / Beachcroft Path loop — ~2.5–3 mi loop, ~1,000 ft of nearly vertical climbing, strenuous. Iron rungs, narrow ledges, short ladders, genuine exposure — the most thrilling non‑technical climb in the park. Summit Champlain (1,058 ft), then descend the gentler Bear Brook or Beachcroft stone staircase back to the loop road.
  • Precipice is one‑way up — never descend it. Not suitable if wet or windy.

If you’re not feeling the rungs

  • Beachcroft Path up Champlain (out‑and‑back) — ~2.4 mi round trip, ~830 ft gain, moderate. A beautifully engineered stone staircase (hundreds of granite steps, built in 1915) delivers you to the same Champlain summit views as Precipice, minus any iron rungs, ladders, or exposure. This is the most satisfying “gentler” substitute in the park.
  • Even easier option: Great Head Trail (1.7 mi loop from Sand Beach, ~150 ft gain) — ocean cliffs, open pink granite, almost no climbing. Pair it with the Ocean Path cool‑down for a satisfying coastal day.

Afternoon / Evening

  • Cool‑down: Ocean Path stroll from Sand Beach to Otter Point (~4 mi round trip, flat, paved). Thunder Hole is loudest 1–2 hours before high tide.
  • Popover dinner at Jordan Pond House with tea on the lawn.
Day 3 · Acadia

Wednesday, September 2 · The big ridge: Penobscot & Sargent

Acadia’s marquee long hike — two summits, one classic loop.

Driving: Short drive to Jordan Pond parking; everything else on foot.

Main Hike — Strenuous

  • Jordan Pond → Jordan Cliffs Trail → Penobscot Mountain → Sargent Mountain → Deer Brook Trail back to Jordan Pond — ~6.2 mi loop, ~1,500 ft gain, strenuous. Sargent (1,373 ft) is the park’s second‑highest peak with a wide, bald summit and 360° views from Frenchman Bay to the western mountains.
  • Swim stop at Sargent Mountain Pond — a tiny glacial tarn about 100 yards below the summit. Nippy but legendary.
  • Extension (9–10 mi): descend the Maple Spring / Giant Slide Trail off Sargent to loop back through quieter western woods and bag Gilmore Peak along the way.

If you’re not feeling a 6‑mile ridge

  • Moderate substitute — Acadia Mountain + St. Sauveur loop (west side of the island): ~4.5 mi, ~900 ft gain, moderate. Summit two small peaks with big views over Somes Sound (the only fjard on the U.S. east coast), finish with a picnic at Echo Lake Beach. Much quieter than the east side.
  • Easy recovery day — Jordan Pond Loop + South Bubble: 3.3 mi flat pond loop plus a short steep climb (200 ft, ~30 min) for the iconic Bubble Rock view. Total ~4 mi, easy‑to‑moderate. Earns you the same popovers.
  • Even easier — rent bikes in Bar Harbor and ride a section of the Rockefeller carriage roads (the Eagle Lake loop is a great intro; crushed gravel, no cars, stone bridges).

Evening

  • Post‑hike popovers back at Jordan Pond House (you’ve earned them).
  • Dinner options: Thurston’s Lobster Pound in Bernard (40 min drive, worth it) or Geddy’s / Galyn’s back in Bar Harbor.
Tip: Carry at least 2L of water per person — there are no reliable water sources on the Penobscot / Sargent ridge. Plan on ~5 hours for the standard loop, 6–7 for the extension.
Day 4 · Travel

Thursday, September 3 · Bar Harbor → North Conway, NH

Optional sunrise stretch, then goodbye coast, hello White Mountains.

Driving: ~4.5 hours / 270 miles via I‑95 and Rt 302.

Optional morning

  • If your legs still have something: Great Head Trail (1.7 mi loop, ocean cliffs from Sand Beach, ~45 min).
  • If not: a coffee and a stroll on the Shore Path in town before you hit the road.

On the road

  • Breakfast in Bar Harbor, wheels rolling by 9:30am.
  • Lunch detour in Portland, ME: Duckfat (Belgian fries, duck confit panini), Eventide Oyster Co. (famous brown‑butter lobster roll), or Holy Donut (potato doughnuts).
  • Optional short stop at the L.L. Bean flagship in Freeport (open 24/7).
  • Arrive North Conway mid to late afternoon. If you’re early, walk the easy Diana’s Baths trail (1.2 mi, flat, waterfalls).
  • Dinner at Moat Mountain Smokehouse & Brewing Co.
Tip: Rt 302 from Fryeburg into Conway is a pretty two‑lane alternative to I‑95 for the last hour — lakes, woods, less highway grind.
Day 5 · White Mountains

Friday, September 4 · White Mountains hiking day

Notches, waterfalls, and a brewery finish.

Driving: Local only; optional Kancamagus Highway loop ~1.5 hours.

Pick one hike

  • Easy payoff: Mt. Willard (3.2 mi round trip, 900 ft gain) — unbelievable Crawford Notch view for minimal effort.
  • Moderate ridgeline: Welch‑Dickey Loop (4.5 mi, 1,800 ft gain) — open ledges, exposed granite, one of the best moderate hikes in the Whites.
  • All‑day challenge: Falling Waters → Little Haystack ridge (8.9 mi, 3,800 ft gain) — above‑treeline Franconia Ridge views, bucket‑list stuff.

Scenic drive option

  • Drive the Kancamagus Highway with stops at Sabbaday Falls and Rocky Gorge.

Evening

  • Coffee or ice cream at Frontside Grind or Zeb’s General Store.
  • Dinner at Tuckerman Brewing Co. or May Kelly’s Cottage for Irish‑leaning comfort food.
Tip: Weather in the Whites changes fast. Check the higher summits forecast and pack layers even in September.
Day 6 · Stowe

Saturday, September 5 · North Conway → Stowe, VT

Through Franconia Notch to the heart of Vermont.

Driving: ~3 hours / 150 miles via I‑93 and I‑89.

On the way

  • Morning stop at Franconia Notch State Park for the Flume Gorge (2‑mile boardwalk loop through a granite chasm; $18/adult).
  • Optional lunch detour at Ben & Jerry’s factory in Waterbury (reserve the tour ahead on weekends).
  • Drive through Smugglers’ Notch on your way into Stowe — narrow, twisty, closed to RVs and trucks, and spectacular.

Afternoon & evening

  • Tasting room hop: The Alchemist (Heady Topper) in Stowe, then von Trapp Brewing for Austrian‑style lagers with an Alpine view.
  • Dinner at Doc Ponds (gastropub, fantastic beer list) or Plate (elevated farm‑to‑table).
Tip: The Alchemist is small and can sell out of Heady Topper — check their site for daily release info.
Day 7 · Adirondacks

Sunday, September 6 · Stowe → Lake Placid, NY

Ferry across Champlain to Olympic country.

Driving: ~3 hours / 130 miles incl. the Lake Champlain ferry.

Morning

  • A quick walk on the Stowe Recreation Path (flat, paved, along the river) or tackle a piece of the Pinnacle Trail for a quick summit before you leave Vermont.

Crossing

  • Burlington → Port Kent Lake Champlain Ferry (~1 hour, runs hourly in season). Reserve a car spot in advance — it’s a holiday weekend.

Afternoon & evening

  • Arrive Lake Placid late afternoon. Walk the Mirror Lake Loop (2.7 mi, flat, town views).
  • Visit the Olympic Center and the 1980 “Miracle on Ice” rink — even a quick look is worth it.
  • Dinner at Lake Placid Pub & Brewery on Mirror Lake.
Backup plan: If weather cancels the ferry (rare but possible), the drive south via the Crown Point Bridge adds ~1 hour.
Day 8 · Finger Lakes

Monday, September 7 · Lake Placid → Finger Lakes

Longest drive of the trip — broken up with one great stop.

Driving: ~5 hours / 290 miles via I‑87 and I‑90 (with a stop).

Midday stop — pick one

  • Option A — Saratoga Springs (~2 hrs from Lake Placid): stroll Broadway, lunch at Hattie’s Chicken Shack or Druthers Brewing, then walk through Congress Park. Note: Labor Day is the final day of the Saratoga Race Course meet — expect traffic and crowds but also a huge atmosphere.
  • Option B — Cooperstown (adds ~45 min total): Baseball Hall of Fame, lunch at the Fly Creek Cider Mill or Ommegang Brewery.

Evening

  • Push on to Seneca Lake (Geneva or Watkins Glen) by evening.
  • Dinner at FLX Table or Kindred Fare in Geneva, or Graft Wine + Cider in Watkins Glen.
Heads up: September 7 is Labor Day. Plan on bigger Saratoga crowds and a busier ferry the day before. Lock in lunch and dinner reservations ahead.
Day 9 · Rochester

Tuesday, September 8 · Finger Lakes → Rochester

Wine trail morning, easy drive into Rochester.

Driving: ~1.5 hours / 70 miles.

Morning options

  • Gorge morning: Watkins Glen State Park gorge trail (1.5 mi one‑way, 19 waterfalls, 832 stone steps — iconic).
  • Wine morning: a Seneca Lake trio — Hermann J. Wiemer (world‑class Riesling), Ravines Wine Cellars, and Lamoreaux Landing.

Lunch & drive

  • Lunch at FLX Wienery in Dundee (hot dogs done shockingly well) or Dano’s Heuriger on Seneca.
  • Easy drive to Rochester via I‑90, or the slower Rt 5/20 if you want to stretch it out.
Tip: Most Finger Lakes wineries will ship bottles home via UPS so you don’t have to haul them through the rest of your trip.

Lodging Suggestions

Early September is still peak season — book as soon as you can, especially Bar Harbor and Stowe. Labor Day weekend falls Sept 5–7, so the Stowe and Lake Placid nights overlap with the holiday.

Bar Harbor, ME — 4 nights (Aug 30, 31, Sept 1, Sept 2)

  • Bar Harbor Inn — oceanfront, classic, walk to town. Mid/high‑end.
  • Bluenose Inn — hilltop views, heated pool, slightly quieter. Mid/high.
  • Acadia Hotel — simple and central in downtown Bar Harbor. Mid.
  • Wonder View Inn — motel‑style, great value, water views. Budget/mid.

North Conway, NH — 2 nights (Sept 3, Sept 4)

  • White Mountain Hotel & Resort — dramatic Moat Mountain views, pool. Mid/high.
  • Red Jacket Mountain View Resort — big family resort feel. Mid.
  • The Eastern Slope Inn — historic, right in North Conway village. Mid.

Stowe, VT — 1 night (Sept 5, Labor Day weekend)

  • Topnotch Resort — spa resort with excellent dining. High.
  • Lodge at Spruce Peak (Stowe Mountain Lodge) — on the mountain, ski‑village vibe. High.
  • Green Mountain Inn — classic country inn in the village. Mid.
  • Field Guide Lodge — boutique, design‑forward, great value. Mid.

Lake Placid, NY — 1 night (Sept 6)

  • Mirror Lake Inn — lakefront, spa, white‑tablecloth dining. High.
  • Golden Arrow Lakeside Resort — on Mirror Lake with a private beach. Mid.
  • High Peaks Resort — central, mountain views. Mid.

Finger Lakes / Seneca Lake — 1 night (Sept 7)

  • Geneva on the Lake — Italianate villa on Seneca Lake, historic. Mid/high.
  • The Lake House on Canandaigua — stunning newer property, worth the slight detour. High.
  • Watkins Glen Harbor Hotel — waterfront, walkable to the gorge. Mid.
  • Inn at Glenora Wine Cellars — stay at a winery on the lake. Mid.

Book Ahead (Do These Now)

  • Cadillac Mountain sunrise vehicle reservation — opens 90 days ahead, sells out fast.
  • Bar Harbor lodging (4 nights) — peak season, book 2–3 months out.
  • Stowe lodging (1 night, Labor Day weekend) — lock this in early; Saturday Sept 5 is a high‑demand night.
  • Lake Champlain ferry (Burlington → Port Kent, Sept 6) — reserve a vehicle spot, especially the day after Labor Day weekend.
  • Ben & Jerry’s factory tour (optional, but books up weekends).
  • Dinner reservations: Jordan Pond House, FLX Table, and any Saturday/Labor‑Day‑weekend dinners on the route.
  • Rental car at Bangor for the week. One‑way drop in Rochester may add cost — compare vs. round trip if someone in Rochester can help.

Packing Checklist

Early September in the Northeast: 45–75°F range, cool mornings, occasional rain, possibly hot afternoons. Pack for layers.

Hiking & Outdoors

  • Broken‑in hiking boots or sturdy trail shoes
  • Moisture‑wicking hiking socks (3+ pairs)
  • Rain jacket / shell (lightweight, packable)
  • Fleece or light insulating layer (Cadillac sunrise is cold)
  • Hiking pants / convertibles and quick‑dry shorts
  • Base‑layer long sleeves (1–2)
  • Sun hat and warm beanie (yes, both)
  • Sunglasses and sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Day pack (20–30L) with rain cover
  • Reusable water bottles or hydration bladder (2L)
  • Trekking poles (optional; useful for Penobscot‑Sargent and Welch‑Dickey)
  • Bug spray and bear‑resistant snack bag
  • Headlamp (for Cadillac pre‑dawn and just in case)
  • Small first‑aid kit + blister care (moleskin, KT tape)

Everyday & Travel

  • Casual clothing for 10 days (assume one laundry stop)
  • One nicer outfit (for Topnotch, FLX Table, or Mirror Lake Inn)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for towns
  • Swimsuit (hotel pools, Sargent Mountain Pond, maybe Mirror Lake)
  • Sleepwear and underwear
  • Toiletries and any prescription meds
  • Reusable tote bag (wineries, farmers’ markets)

Car & Road Trip

  • Phone car mount and charging cables (USB‑C / Lightning)
  • Offline maps downloaded — cell service is spotty in the Whites and Adirondacks
  • Paper road atlas as backup
  • Cooler or soft‑sided tote for brewery/winery finds
  • Road snacks, coffee mugs, bottled water
  • EZ‑Pass transponder (useful on I‑90 through NY)
  • Rental car and insurance paperwork
  • Trash bag for the car

Documents & Essentials

  • Driver’s license and credit cards
  • Acadia park pass confirmation + Cadillac reservation printout
  • Lodging confirmations (printed or offline PDFs)
  • Flight and rental car confirmations
  • Ferry reservation confirmation
  • Cash for small‑town stops that are still cash‑only

Want to Tweak the Plan?

  • Stay near BGR on Sunday night: if you’d rather not drive the hour after a transcontinental flight, overnight at the Four Points by Sheraton or Hilton Garden Inn Bangor and drive to Bar Harbor Monday morning — trade one Bar Harbor night for one near the airport.
  • More Vermont, less New York: add a second night in Stowe and skip Lake Placid — drive straight to the Finger Lakes on Day 8.
  • More coast: swap Day 4 lunch for an overnight in Portland, ME; move the White Mountains to a shorter Day 5.
  • Stretch to 11 days: add a Saratoga Springs or Cooperstown overnight on Day 8 to break up the longest drive.
  • More Acadia miles if you’re hungry: swap Day 1’s warm‑up for the Cadillac South Ridge Trail (7 mi round trip from Rt 3, ~1,300 ft gain) — summit Cadillac on foot and skip the sunrise reservation.
  • Quieter Acadia option: trade one day for the west side — Acadia Mountain + St. Sauveur loop (4.5 mi, moderate) and a picnic at Echo Lake. Half the crowds, same rock.
  • Bad weather fallback: if rungs are closed or wet, substitute the Beachcroft stone staircase up Champlain (Day 2) and the Penobscot Mountain Trail avoiding Jordan Cliffs (Day 3).
  • Easier hiking in NH: trade Welch‑Dickey for Mt. Willard in the Whites.
