How Phoenix Compares to the Alps

The TMB's actual col-climbing sections (stripped of flat valley approaches) run 565–850 ft/mi for stretches of 2.5–5+ miles. Phoenix's hard trails hit that same steepness — but only for 1.3–3 miles at a time. Phoenix training rewards multiple laps and combined-trail days over any single out-and-back.

The raw altitude of the TMB (cols at 7,000–8,700 ft) cannot be replicated in the Phoenix metro. The highest trailhead here is ~2,800 ft. Plan for separate altitude acclimatization if possible before the TMB. What Phoenix can train is gradient tolerance, leg strength, and descent conditioning.

Three Cornerstone Training Hikes

★ Monthly Benchmark
Siphon Draw → The Flatiron
2.8 mi one way  |  ~2,800–3,100 ft gain  |  5–6 hrs RT

The only Phoenix-area trail approximating alpine col distance and character. Do monthly to measure progress. Start by 5:00 am from May onward.

★ Weekly Staple
Tom's Thumb
2.0 mi one way  |  ~1,200–1,400 ft gain  |  ~2.5 hrs RT

Gradient lands squarely in the TMB col range (600–700 ft/mi). Feels most like alpine hiking. Add pack weight progressively starting in week 6.

★ Monthly Long Day
South Mountain — National Trail
15.4 mi point-to-point  |  ~2,273 ft gain  |  4–5+ hrs

Builds the sustained endurance needed to walk 8–12 miles every day for 11 consecutive days. Do 8–12 miles, not necessarily the full traverse.

The Trails

Siphon Draw → The Flatiron

Lost Dutchman State Park, Apache Junction ~45 min east of central Phoenix
Distance~2.8 mi one way
Gain~2,800–3,100 ft
Gradient (one way)~1,000 ft/mi avg
Elevation2,060 → 4,861 ft
Round trip time5–6 hours
Character: The first 2 miles follow a maintained trail at roughly 400 ft/mi. The final 0.8 miles to the Flatiron is unmaintained, involves boulder scrambling, and gains the majority of elevation at an extreme pitch. Trail is marked to Siphon Draw; above that point navigation judgment is required.
TMB Analog
The approach section (first 2 miles at ~400 ft/mi) simulates Stage 9 (Col de Balme, 433 ft/mi avg) and the Stage 3 col climb (~738 ft/mi for 4.3 miles). The full route is the closest single-trail simulation of a hard TMB col day available in the Phoenix metro.

Heat note: Start by 5:00–5:30 am from May onward. Higher elevation makes this marginally cooler than Camelback/Piestewa, but still dangerous in summer. Bring minimum 3 liters of water.

Camelback Mountain — Echo Canyon Trail

Phoenix Mountains / Paradise Valley Central Phoenix
Distance1.3 mi one way
Gain~1,490 ft
Gradient (one way)~1,120 ft/mi
Elevation1,362 → 2,704 ft
Round trip time1.5–2.5 hrs
Character: Railroad ties and steel rails in the first third; exposed rock scramble in the last half-mile. No flat relief — relentlessly steep throughout. Parking fills by 6:30 am on weekends.
TMB Analog — Climbing
Steeper than any single TMB col. Best for Stage 10 preparation (Tré le Champ → Flégère, 481 ft/mi, iron ladders). Do two complete laps (down and back up) to reach Stage 10's ~2,400 ft of total gain.
TMB Analog — Descent Training
The Echo Canyon descent (~1,120 ft/mi down) mirrors the "never-ending, jarring" Stage 11 descent (845 ft/mi) and Stage 6's knee-testing Swiss descent. Essential conditioning for protecting your knees over 11 consecutive days.

Heat note: Start by 6:00 am in summer. Full southern exposure — dangerous by 9 am in June–September.

Piestewa Peak — Summit Trail #300

Phoenix Mountains Preserve Central Phoenix
Distance1.2 mi one way
Gain~1,200 ft
Gradient (one way)~1,000 ft/mi
Elevation~1,400 → 2,610 ft
Round trip time1–2 hrs
Character: Relentless with no switchbacks and no flat sections anywhere on the trail. The most consistently steep gradient in Phoenix. Foot traffic only (no bikes or dogs). Single-file sections near the top.
TMB Analog — Multi-Lap Training
The Col du Bonhomme / Croix du Bonhomme climbs in Stage 2 (~690 ft/mi for 5.6 miles sustained) set the benchmark for sustained alpine climbing. Piestewa is steeper but much shorter — doing 3 complete round trips in a single session matches Stage 2's ~3,900 ft of gain in the climbing sections. Brutal but highly effective interval training.

Heat note: Start by 6:00 am in summer. Exposed rock absorbs significant heat by mid-morning.

Camelback Mountain — Cholla Trail

Scottsdale / Paradise Valley East approach to Camelback summit
Distance~1.5 mi one way
Gain~1,350 ft
Gradient (one way)~918 ft/mi
Round trip time2–3 hrs
Character: More gradual than Echo Canyon for the first mile, then technical scrambling near the summit. Fully exposed with minimal shade. A useful introduction to Camelback's steepness before attempting Echo Canyon.
TMB Analog
Stage 1's Col de Voza climb (~850 ft/mi for 2.5 miles). Cholla is steeper but shorter. Combine Cholla and Echo in a single day — up Cholla, down Echo — for a well-rounded Camelback session.

Heat note: Same as Echo Canyon — fully exposed, start by 6:00 am in summer.

Tom's Thumb Trail

McDowell Sonoran Preserve, Scottsdale Trailhead at ~2,820 ft — highest in Phoenix metro
Distance2.0 mi one way
Gain~1,200–1,400 ft
Gradient (one way)~609–705 ft/mi
Elevation2,820 → 3,848 ft
Round trip time~2.5 hrs
Character: A proper hiking trail — sustained, consistent climb through desert and granite terrain, without technical scrambling. The effort profile most closely resembles walking a real alpine trail. The granite thumb formation is the obvious landmark at the summit.
TMB Analog — Best Phoenix Match for Col Climbing
This is the best gradient match for the moderate col sections: Stage 6's Grand Col Ferret climb (~672 ft/mi for 2.5 miles) and Stage 8's Alp Bovine climb (~565 ft/mi for 3.1 miles). The gradient, trail character, and sustained effort most closely simulate what you'll do on the TMB. Make this your primary weekly training trail.

Heat note: Highest elevation in the metro — somewhat cooler than Camelback/Piestewa. Start by 6:30 am in summer.

Sunrise Peak

McDowell Mountain Regional Park, Fountain Hills
Distance~1.9 mi one way
Gain~1,140 ft
Gradient (one way)~600 ft/mi
Elevation~1,960 → 3,069 ft
Round trip time~2 hrs
Character: Sustained desert climb on a proper trail. Similar family to Tom's Thumb — consistent gradient without scrambling. Good views of the McDowell range and surrounding valley.
TMB Analog
Stage 8's Alp Bovine climb (~565 ft/mi). Combine Sunrise Peak + Tom's Thumb on the same day for a Stage 3 simulation — the Col de la Seigne is 3,173 ft over 4.3 miles, and these two together give ~2,300–2,500 ft in the same gradient range.

Wind Cave Trail — Usery Mountain Regional Park

Mesa / East Valley
Distance~1.5 mi one way
Gain~800–1,000 ft
Gradient (one way)~535–667 ft/mi
Round trip time1.5–2 hrs
Character: Short, steady climb on a maintained trail. Less crowded than the Camelback/Piestewa corridor. Good option for training variety. Works well combined with other trails in the east Valley.
TMB Analog
A moderately hard day, closest to Stage 5 (Courmayeur → Bonatti, 350–400 ft/mi sustained throughout). Good for a training day when legs are tired from the previous session.

South Mountain — National Trail

South Mountain Park, South Phoenix 15.4 miles point-to-point — do a section, not full traverse
Full distance15.4 mi point-to-point
Full gain~2,273 ft
Avg gradient~148 ft/mi
Recommended section8–12 miles
Character: Long ridgeline trail with rolling terrain — moderate ups and downs throughout. The gradient average is low, but the distance demands sustained effort. Not about steepness; about time on feet and consecutive-day conditioning.
TMB Analog
Stage 7 (La Fouly → Champex-Lac, 148 ft/mi — the TMB's recovery day) and the rolling moderate sections of Stage 11. The primary value here is distance endurance: building the base to walk 8–12 miles every day for 11 consecutive days.

Heat note: Low elevation — among the hottest training options in summer. Start by 5:30 am in summer months.

Phoenix Heat Management

The TMB is walked at altitude in cool alpine air. Phoenix trains your legs and lungs, not your heat tolerance. Manage training conditions deliberately.

  • October–April: Near-ideal conditions. Flexible start times for most trails.
  • May–June: Hot and getting hotter. Start all exposed trails (Camelback, Piestewa, South Mountain) before 7:00 am. Siphon Draw before 6:00 am.
  • July–September: Dangerous heat on lower trails. Start Camelback/Piestewa by 5:30–6:00 am maximum. Siphon Draw / Flatiron (higher elevation, ~5,000 ft) can start slightly later but still requires very early starts. Monsoon season begins mid-July — watch for afternoon lightning at elevation and flash flood risk in canyon drainages.
  • Water: Carry 1 liter per hour as a minimum — desert dehydration is faster than alpine dehydration. Electrolytes matter more here than on the cool TMB; train with them.
  • Gear: Sun protection (hat, sleeves, sunscreen) is as important as the right boots. Your TMB gear will be heavier than necessary for Phoenix — this is fine; train with it.
